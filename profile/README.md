Form Commons is an open initiative for creating **accessible, adaptable, and maintainable documents** used in regulated public-interest work.

Many agencies, service providers, nonprofits, contractors, and public programs must produce documents whose content is shaped by laws, regulations, funding rules, contracts, accessibility standards, and professional guidance. Too often, those documents are recreated independently, maintained inconsistently, and made difficult for the people using them to understand.

**Form Commons develops shared foundations for doing this work better.**

> 🏗️ We treat important public-service documents as shared infrastructure - not isolated files.

## 🛠️ What we build

Our projects may include:

- 📄 openly licensed document templates;
- 🗺️ jurisdiction-specific editions and compliance crosswalks;
- ♿ plain-language and accessibility guidance;
- 🧱 reusable document components and design systems;
- 🧬 structured source schemas for regulated documents;
- ⚙️ tools for generating DOCX, ODT, PDF, and web-based editions;
- ✅ validation, testing, and versioning infrastructure;
- 📘 implementation guidance for agencies and service providers.

The goal is not to replace legal, regulatory, or professional review. It is to turn public requirements into **clearer, more usable, and more maintainable document infrastructure**.

## 🌱 Our principles

### 👤 Person-centered and usable

Documents should serve the people whose lives they describe—not only auditors, administrators, or software systems.

### ♿ Accessible by default

Accessibility, plain language, cognitive usability, screen-reader compatibility, and print quality should be designed in from the beginning.

### 🔎 Requirements are traceable

Jurisdiction-specific content should be connected to current primary sources, with clear verification dates, implementation notes, and confidence levels.

### 🔓 Open and adaptable

Organizations should be able to inspect, reuse, localize, and improve the work without being locked into a proprietary platform.

### 🧩 Structured, not duplicated

Shared components, declarative source files, and jurisdiction overlays should reduce copy-and-paste drift across document editions.

### 🔐 Privacy-aware

Person-facing material, clinical details, administrative records, and restricted information should be separated according to purpose and access needs.

### 🧭 Honest about scope

No single document can satisfy every jurisdiction or replace every mandatory government form. Each project should state where it can be used, where it supplements an official system, and where further review is required.

## 🚀 Initial focus

Our first project is an openly licensed framework for **person-centered support plans** and **provider implementation documents** used in intellectual and developmental disability services.

The project is being designed to support:

- a universal person-centered core;
- U.S. Medicaid HCBS requirements;
- provider-level implementation plans;
- jurisdiction-specific overlays;
- accessible and Easy Read editions;
- DOCX, ODT, PDF, and future digital formats;
- transparent regulatory crosswalks and release history.

## 🧪 Project status

Form Commons is in its early development stage.

Initial work is focused on:

1. establishing the document architecture and design system;
2. building a reproducible document-generation toolchain;
3. publishing the first support-plan framework;
4. validating accessibility and usability with real stakeholders;
5. developing carefully scoped jurisdiction-specific editions.

Repositories and releases should clearly identify their status:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 🧪 **Experimental** | Early work that may change substantially |
| 🟡 **Candidate** | Ready for focused review and pilot use |
| 🟢 **Verified edition** | Reviewed against identified primary sources through a stated date |
| 🧷 **Supplement-only** | Designed to accompany—not replace—an official form or system |
| 🗄️ **Deprecated** | Superseded and no longer recommended |

A **verified** label does not constitute legal advice or a guarantee of compliance.

## 🤝 Who this is for

Form Commons may be useful to:

- self-advocates and people receiving services;
- families and supporters;
- public agencies;
- nonprofit and community-based organizations;
- human-services providers;
- support coordinators and case-management entities;
- compliance and quality-assurance teams;
- accessibility specialists;
- policy researchers;
- legal and regulatory reviewers;
- document engineers and open-source contributors.

## 💬 Contributing

We welcome contributions from people with lived experience, practitioners, designers, developers, policy specialists, accessibility reviewers, and legal or compliance professionals.

Depending on the project, contributions may include:

- identifying missing or outdated requirements;
- reviewing language for clarity and dignity;
- testing documents with assistive technology;
- improving templates and document components;
- validating jurisdiction crosswalks;
- reporting compatibility issues;
- contributing fictional examples and implementation guidance;
- improving generation, validation, and release tooling.

Each repository will provide its own contribution instructions and review standards.

## 🏛️ Governance and review

Form Commons aims to use transparent, documented processes for:

- source verification;
- editorial review;
- accessibility review;
- release approval;
- change tracking;
- deprecation;
- community feedback;
- conflict-of-interest disclosure.

Major projects should seek review from affected communities and subject-matter experts before being represented as stable or jurisdiction-ready.

## 📜 Licensing

Unless a repository states otherwise:

- document templates and written guidance are intended to be released under **Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0)**;
- software, schemas, and developer tooling are intended to use a permissive open-source license such as **Apache-2.0** or **MIT**.

Always consult the license included with the specific repository or release.

## ⚠️ Important notice

Form Commons provides open document infrastructure and research-informed implementation resources. It is **not** a government agency, law firm, certification body, or substitute for legal advice.

Organizations remain responsible for confirming that a document meets current laws, regulations, contracts, payer requirements, agency policies, accessibility obligations, and recordkeeping rules that apply to their work.
